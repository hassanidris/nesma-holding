Since its establishment, Nesma has been delivering transformational projects for an advanced infrastructure serving future generations.

SIGNATURE PROJECTS

The projects highlighted below are a few examples of how we have contributed to the development of today’s modern Saudi Arabia in line with Saudi Vision 2030’s pillars for a vibrant society, thriving economy, and ambitious nation.

Jeddah Stormwater Drainage Project

Jeddah
2010-2011
The project was prompted by the deadly Jeddah floods of 2010. It culminated in building essential infrastructure of Jeddah’s stormwater drainage structure. This system prevents the destruction of thousands of homes and other structures and saves lives.

King Abdulaziz Road, Makkah

The Holy City of Makkah
2015
The project alleviates congestion in the Holy City of Makkah. It facilitates religious tourism providing access to pilgrims. It has built and installed state-of-the-art and future technologies in transportation, including 2 metro stations, a 5,700-meter metro tunnel, 4 carparks, 2 flyovers, 11 pedestrian underpasses, 12 vehicle underpasses, 2 bus stations, and South and North tunnels.

National Guard Health Affairs Specialized Hospitals

Riyadh, Jeddah, Al Taif, and Al Qassim
2014
- The project is assigned to build 5 new specialized Saudi Arabian National Guard Health Affairs hospitals.
- Hospitals will serve an additional 1,400 citizens per day in specialized women and children care, and neuroscience and trauma facilities.

Nesma Airlines Ha’il

Ha’il, Jeddah, and Riyadh
2016- present
- Nesma Airlines is based in Ha’il since 2016.
- It operates 12 domestic routes and regular service between Jeddah and Riyadh.
- It is fundamental to the strategic social and economic development of the region, contributing to the Kingdom’s GDP and creating hundreds of new jobs.

Makkah Bus Project

Makkah
2017- present
- A consortium made of Nesma Co. Ltd and the Spanish transport company Transports Ciutat Comtal (TCC) was awarded the contract.
- The project supplies the 10-year operation and maintenance of 400 Makkah Public Transportation Program buses.
- It will ultimately provide wider transportation options for the public and improve travel throughout the city while addressing the social, economic and environmental impacts of transport.
